UNIT-I: ACTIVITIES ON LISTENING AND READING COMPREHENSION

1. What is Listening?

Hearing

Effortless act of perceiving sound through ears.


Listening

Active process of understanding and extracting meaning from sound.

Requires concentration and practice.


How to Start Listening?

Identify different layers of sound.

Focus on speech, background noise, and other sounds.

Analyze voice characteristics and overall impression.


2. Tools to Develop Listening Skills

1. Headphones

Improve clarity of speech content.


2. Digital Audio Workstation (DAW)

Used for recording and analyzing audio.


3. Place to Take Notes

Helps describe and remember what is heard.


4. Take Breaks

Prevents ear fatigue and improves effectiveness.


3. What is Active Listening?

Paying full attention to the speaker.

Demonstrating understanding and empathy.

Helps retain information and build relationships.


Benefits of Active Listening

Makes people feel heard.

Builds empathy and relationships.

Improves memory and conflict resolution.

Useful in career and workplace communication.


Active Listening Techniques

1. Focus on Intent and Purpose

Give complete attention to the speaker.


2. Pay Attention to Body Language

Observe facial expressions, posture, and eye contact.


3. Give Encouraging Verbal Cues

Use responses like "Yes", "I see", and "I understand".


4. Clarify and Paraphrase Information

Repeat information to confirm understanding.


5. Ask Questions

Remove confusion and show interest.


6. Refrain from Judgment

Remain open-minded and unbiased.


7. Summarize, Share, and Reflect

Review important points at the end of conversation.

7. What is a Discourse Marker?

Words and phrases used to connect ideas and organize speech.

Helps express feelings and attitudes.

Improves fluency and communication.


Functions of Discourse Markers

1. Sequencing Information

Examples:

First

Firstly

Secondly

Finally

Later


2. Adding Information

Examples:

Also

Additionally

Besides

Another reason


3. Comparing

Examples:

Similarly

Likewise

In the same way


4. Giving Examples

Examples:

For example

For instance

Such as

A clear example is


8. Importance of Discourse Markers

Improves fluency.

Engages listeners.

Reduces fillers such as "umm" and "err".

Enhances communication skills and English proficiency.
